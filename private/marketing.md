# 2. Marketing

## Mission

Generate qualified demand for upull.ai and build the brand's public presence, measured against clear KPIs rather than vanity output.

## 90-day plan

- Weeks 1–2: Nail positioning and messaging; set up a simple content calendar and analytics baseline (site traffic, sign-ups).

- Weeks 3–6: Publish weekly content (blog/LinkedIn), launch one lead-gen offer (guide, demo, waitlist).

- Weeks 7–10: Run one paid or partnership experiment; double down on whichever channel shows the best cost-per-lead.

- Weeks 11–13: Package a repeatable monthly content + campaign engine; report on what drove signed customers, not just clicks.

## KPIs and targets

| KPI | Month 1 | Month 2 | Month 3 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Website visitors | 500 | 1,200 | 2,500 |
| Content pieces published | 4 | 6 | 8 |
| Leads / sign-ups | 20 | 60 | 120 |
| Lead → Sales handoff | 5 | 15 | 30 |
| Cost per lead | n/a (organic) | < £15 | < £12 |


## Weekly cadence for the Marketing project

- Every Monday: content plan for the week, campaign status, and lead numbers vs target.

- Flag anything worth the CEO's input on brand direction, tone, or a paid spend decision.

### Project custom instructions (copy-paste into 'Set project instructions')

You are the Marketing lead for upull.ai. You report weekly to the CEO.

Your job: content planning, copywriting, campaign ideas, and tracking KPIs against the targets in the knowledge base.

Every Monday, produce a 'This Week' plan: content to ship, campaigns running, KPI snapshot vs. target, and any decisions you need from the CEO.

Be honest when a channel isn't working — recommend cutting it rather than continuing for volume's sake.
