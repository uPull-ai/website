# 3. Admin

## Mission

Keep the company's operational backbone tidy: documentation, events, records, and the paperwork every other team relies on. This team supports all the others rather than owning its own revenue or growth number.

## Core responsibilities

- Maintain a single, current source of truth for company documents (policies, templates, contracts index).

- Own event planning: internal (team syncs, offsites) and external (demos, meetups, conferences).

- Keep documentation updates flowing — when Sales, Marketing, Finance, or Engineering produce something worth keeping, Admin files and indexes it.

- Track action items and follow-ups across teams so nothing falls through the cracks between the CEO and the departments.

## 90-day plan

- Weeks 1–2: Set up a shared document structure (naming conventions, folders, a company handbook skeleton).

- Weeks 3–6: Build templates other teams reuse — meeting notes, proposal template, onboarding checklist.

- Weeks 7–10: Plan and run the first company event or external touchpoint (e.g. a demo day or webinar).

- Weeks 11–13: Audit documentation coverage across all teams and close the gaps.

## Weekly cadence for the Admin project

- Every Monday: status of open documentation tasks, upcoming events/deadlines, and a consolidated follow-up list pulled from the other teams.

## Meetings, documents & diary tracking — working setup

- The CEO holds recurring meetings with others every 2 weeks via Google Meet.
- Google account in use: **pyacheung**
- Admin's job here: keep a running log of meetings (date, attendees, outcomes/action items), track document updates across teams, and manage the diary/calendar of upcoming meetings and deadlines.
- When calendar/document connectors are available, use them to pull meeting details automatically rather than relying on manual updates; until then, log meetings here as they happen.

## Cross-team templates owned by Admin

Per the plan's 90-day instruction to "build templates other teams reuse," Admin maintains the following, each as a standalone file for the relevant team's Project Knowledge:

- **`admin-template-case-study.md`** — for Engineering, to standardise how real trust case studies get collected and sourced before publishing (see Engineering's case study pipeline notes on avoiding fabricated/placeholder content).
- **`admin-template-podcast-episode.md`** — for Growth & Social, tracking each episode from the 30-episode plan through production and its 3-post LinkedIn repurposing.
- **`admin-template-marketing-campaign.md`** — for Marketing, a standard campaign brief tied to the KPIs already set in the Marketing plan.
- **`admin-template-sales-meeting.md`** — for Sales, standardising how meetings get scheduled and prepped, cross-referenced against the NHS stakeholder list.

Admin's job: keep these templates current, chase teams to actually use them (rather than letting them go stale), and flag in the weekly report if a team isn't filling theirs in.
