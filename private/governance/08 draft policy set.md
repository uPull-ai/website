# 8. Draft Policy Set

*Part of the uPull.ai Public Sector Supplier Requirement Pack. See `00-overview.md` for the legal review note — this is not legal advice. These are drafting starting points, not finished policies — each should be reviewed by a qualified UK solicitor before adoption or submission.*

Six policies uPull.ai should hold, with a summary of what each should cover.

## Governance and delegated authority policy
uPull.ai should maintain a simple governance policy that identifies who can approve proposals, discounts, subcontracting, new data uses, security exceptions, and contract signature. For an early-stage company, this can be a founder approval matrix supported by a written conflicts process and a requirement that no public sector proposal proceeds without governance and security review.

## Information security policy
uPull.ai should commit to role-based access control, least privilege, logging, incident escalation, supplier oversight, and documented recovery procedures. The policy should define device controls, password and MFA expectations, backup standards, patching cadence, and secure offboarding for staff and contractors.

## Data protection policy
uPull.ai should define lawful basis assessment, controller-versus-processor analysis, retention limits, deletion rules, subject rights handling, breach reporting, and subprocessor approval steps. The policy should also explain when DPIAs are required and who signs off new processing involving NHS or public sector customer data.

## Anti-bribery and conflicts policy
uPull.ai should prohibit bribery, improper hospitality, undisclosed commissions, and undisclosed personal interests in procurements. It should require declarations from founders, employees, advisors, and subcontractors involved in bids or delivery.

## Equality and respectful business policy
uPull.ai should adopt a short equality, diversity, and non-discrimination policy for employment, subcontracting, and customer delivery. This should also cover accessible communication and non-biased service design where the platform supports public services.

## Modern slavery policy
uPull.ai should publish a policy against forced labour and trafficking in its own operations and supply chain, even if not yet legally required to publish a full annual statement by turnover threshold. Public buyers commonly expect a proportionate modern slavery position from suppliers.
