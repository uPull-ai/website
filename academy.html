Understanding CPD in the uPull.ai Academy

What does CPD mean?

CPD stands for Continuing Professional Development. It is the ongoing, lifelong record of learning, reflection and practical activity that professionals use to maintain, evidence and improve the knowledge and skills needed for their role. Most healthcare and public sector professional bodies — including the GMC, NMC, HCPC and Royal Colleges — expect members to keep an up-to-date CPD record as part of revalidation or continued registration.

How CPD works in this academy

  • Every course and practical task carries a stated number of CPD credits, shown alongside its description, expectation, target audience and level.
  • As a general rule, 1 hour of learning ≈ 1 CPD credit, with additional credit for practical, applied tasks.
  • Signing in records your completed courses and credits to a personal CPD log, which you can carry into appraisal, revalidation or job applications.
  • Credits accumulate toward three recognition tiers — Awareness, Practitioner and Specialist — each with a publicly shareable digital badge.

Recognition, portability and your CV

CPD credits earned here are designed to be portable. Where a course is delivered by a vendor or professional body (for example Microsoft, Google, IBM, the Royal College of Physicians or the NHS Clinical Entrepreneur Programme), any certificate or badge they issue sits alongside your uPull.ai record — so your evidence works in both systems. Over time, uPull.ai aims to map its own credit scheme to recognised third-party CPD accreditors, so your progression counts toward formal revalidation as well as your personal CV and career story.

Free for NHS and public sector colleagues

Courses and CPD tracking in this academy are free for NHS and wider public sector learners. Where a linked third-party course has its own cost, this is shown clearly on the course card before you enrol.
