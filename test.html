What is uPull.ai?
A workflow-led approach to healthcare AI adoption. We help NHS organisations redesign a specific pathway first, then pull the right AI capability into that workflow where it creates measurable value — not the other way around.
What does "Pull" actually mean?
Pull means the workflow defines the requirement. We start with a real operational bottleneck, define the safety envelope, map the people and process changes, then introduce the AI capability needed for that specific job.
Who is this designed for?
NHS trusts, ICS leaders, operational executives, digital transformation teams, frontline service leads, and clinical or operational intrapreneurs who need measurable change — not another abstract AI strategy discussion.
Is this a software product?
It should be understood first as a delivery model. The value comes from selecting the right workflow, creating a governed pilot, building local capability, and proving ROI. Software and AI tools support that process — they are not the starting point.
How long does a Proof of Value take?
The initial Proof of Value runs for six weeks, focused on one workflow with two frontline staff. It is designed to be low-risk, fast, and to generate enough real data to inform whether and how to scale.
What does the Proof of Value involve?
One workflow selected with your team, two frontline staff who are supported and upskilled, a governed AI capability introduced with a defined safety envelope, and measurable outcomes tracked from day one.
How does self-funding work?
The model is built around a 5:1 return framework. The efficiencies captured from the Prove stage generate enough measurable value to fund the Build stage. If the numbers don't stack up, we say so before you proceed.
What happens to staff capability?
Building internal capability is the whole point. By the end of the programme, your team should be able to identify, design, and deliver the next workflow improvement themselves — without needing to re-engage external consultants.
How is AI governance handled?
Every AI capability introduced has a defined safety envelope — the conditions under which it operates, what it cannot do, and how human oversight is maintained. This is defined before deployment, not after.
How do we get started?
Register your organisation's interest on the waitlist. We will review your context and invite you to an initial conversation to assess fit. The first cohort is limited — we work with a small number of organisations at a time to ensure quality.
